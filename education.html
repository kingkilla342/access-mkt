The Dispute Process

4 Rounds of Structured Disputes

Four rounds of formal credit bureau disputes — each one strategically designed to maximize removals and corrections to your credit file.

How Each Round Works

For each round, your ACCESS expert prepares formal dispute letters for all three bureaus, referencing specific federal law violations (FCRA, FDCPA) applicable to each trade line. Credit bureaus have 30–45 days by law to investigate and respond. Each round builds on the results of the last — giving unresolved items escalating legal pressure.

Round 1: Initial dispute letters sent to all three bureaus covering all identified disputable trade lines.
Round 2: Follow-up disputes targeting items not removed, with additional legal arguments and documentation.
Rounds 3 & 4: Escalated dispute strategies using method of verification requests and procedural challenges.
Monthly updates after each bureau response — you're always informed of what's been removed or updated.

Know Your Rights

Federal Laws That Protect You

Federal law gives you powerful rights when it comes to your credit. Here's what you need to know.

CROA
Credit Repair Organizations Act

Requires all credit repair organizations to provide written contracts, prohibits advance fees before services are performed, and gives consumers the right to cancel within 3 business days. ACCESS is fully CROA compliant.

FCRA
Fair Credit Reporting Act

Gives you the right to dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information on your credit report. Credit bureaus must investigate disputes within 30–45 days and correct or remove errors they cannot verify.

FDCPA
Fair Debt Collection Practices Act

Protects consumers from abusive or deceptive debt collection practices. Limits how and when collectors can contact you, and gives you the right to request debt validation in writing.

ECOA
Equal Credit Opportunity Act

Prohibits creditors from discriminating against credit applicants on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or receipt of public assistance.
